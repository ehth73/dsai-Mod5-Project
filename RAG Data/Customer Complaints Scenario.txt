Question: What to do if you lost your debit card
Answer: If you have lost your debit card and shared your OTP, follow these steps to secure your account and report the theft:
Lock your card: Use the online services of your bank to lock your debit card immediately. This stops all new transactions and payments. 
Report the theft: Contact your bank's customer service to report the theft. Provide them with the details of the theft, such as the date, time, and amount of the transaction. 
Request a replacement: After reporting the theft, request a new debit card from your bank. This will help you regain access to your account and prevent unauthorized transactions. 
Check for unauthorized charges: Even after blocking your card, check your bank statement for any unauthorized charges. Report these to your bank as soon as possible.
By following these steps, you can minimize the impact of the theft and protect your account from further unauthorized access.

Answer: Ledger of Quiet Rivers
In marble halls where whispers count, 
the air is thick with patient sums,
coins breathe in vaults, their golden fount,
while time ticks slow on silver drums.
A pen glides smooth as winter rain,
inking the dreams of those who wait;
each signature a fragile chain
between tomorrow and its fate.
The banker’s gaze, a measured tide,
reads currents deep beneath the skin;

and locks it carefully within.
